The brief

Competing with neobanks from the inside

Bank of Queensland acquired Virgin Money Australia in 2013. By 2018, the digital banking landscape was being disrupted by a wave of neobanks — Xinja, 86 400, Volt — all building cloud-native, app-first banking from scratch. They were unburdened by legacy systems, branch networks, and decades of accumulated technical debt. The challenge for BOQ Group was clear: how do you compete with the agility of a neobank while leveraging the scale, capital, and regulatory standing of a 148-year-old institution?

The answer was to use the Virgin Money brand as the vehicle for BOQ’s digital banking transformation — and to build that transformation through a disciplined program of FinTech partnerships, piloted, validated, and scaled through the Innovation function I built from the ground up.

The approach

Building the function that builds the future

I established BOQ’s Innovation function from zero — defining the strategy, governance frameworks, commercial thresholds, and organisational capability required to identify, trial, and scale new opportunities inside a complex, regulated financial services environment. Rather than attempting to build all capability in-house, the team ran structured pilots with globally leading FinTechs, evaluating partners against rigorous commercial and technical criteria before scaling.

Each partnership went through a defined test-and-learn process: a targeted pilot with live customers, validated against commercial viability thresholds, with a business case presented at board and executive level before scaling investment. Two to three validated initiatives reached scale each year.

Key partnerships included Frollo for financial wellbeing and open banking (enabling Australia’s first bank-led Open Banking account aggregation), and Temenos for cloud-native core banking — the infrastructure that gave BOQ and Virgin Money the flexibility of a neobank at institutional scale. Temenos went live for BOQ and Virgin Money in August 2022.

The outcomes

A digital bank delivered at institutional scale

The Virgin Money app launched in 2021 — account opening in under five minutes, real-time Osko payments, PayID, biometric authentication and a Savings Round Up feature. The Lock Saver Feature launched shortly after — a first in Australian banking — allowing customers to lock savings accounts for a defined period and earn a higher interest rate.

BOQ went live on the Temenos Banking Cloud in August 2022 — migrating retail banking operations for both BOQ and Virgin Money onto a single cloud-native platform. As BOQ’s Chief Product and Transformation Officer said at the time: “We are modernising our technology from back to front to give us the flexibility and agility of a neobank, while leveraging the scalability and long-term investment of an established institution.”
